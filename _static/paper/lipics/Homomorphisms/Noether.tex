\subsubsection{Homomorphism decomposition}\label{homomorphism-decomposition}
If \ab g \as : \af{hom} \ab 𝑨 \ab 𝑩, \ab h \as : \af{hom} \ab 𝑨 \ab 𝑪, \ab h is surjective, and \af{ker} \ab h \af{⊆} \af{ker} \ab g, then there exists \ab ϕ \as : \af{hom} \ab 𝑪 \ab 𝑩\as{\}} such that \ab g \as = \ab ϕ \af ∘ \ab h, that is, such that the following diagram commutes.

\begin{verbatim}
𝑨---- h -->>𝑪
 \         .
  \       .
   g     ∃ϕ
    \   .
     \ .
      V
      𝑩
\end{verbatim}
